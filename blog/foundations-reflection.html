What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

What are the role of values, empathy and self awareness in learning and programming?
They don't have a role in programming at all, but programming has a role in humanity. An AI could program with none of these things, but a human needs these things if they want to get anywhere.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?
I don't know, it was a bit of a curve ball. I was surprised how hard the questions are to answer.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?
Writing about things I'm not really that intersted in, and don't really have an opinion on.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?
Because employers want devlopers with human skills; to make things like pair programming more bearable for both parties.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practising programming instead? Justify your answer.
I don't think it's a waste of time, but I'm a very ignorant position. I'm sure Enspiral after all these years have at least some idea of what they're doing, and I don't doubt the value of learning these skills, however I'm going in a little blind to what the end result is supposed to be. It's like learning to read one letter at a time, without ever having seen a book or any kind of text. I think it'll all be more obvious in person, rather than online.
